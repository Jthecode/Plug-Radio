Submission Standards

What artists should include before sending music

The stronger the submission, the easier it is for the station to review, understand the release, and decide whether it fits a show, spotlight, interview, or future programming opportunity.

01

Send the best version of the record

Make sure the title is correct, the link works, and the record is presented cleanly and professionally.

02

Include context around the release

Explain whether this is a single, rollout, project, premiere, interview pitch, or part of a bigger campaign.

03

Add social and contact details

Make it easy for the station team to follow up, tag the artist properly, and understand the brand behind the music.

Best Practices

What helps a submission stand out

  • Clear artist name and track title
  • One strong primary music link
  • Professional cover art or release context
  • Instagram handle and active contact email
  • A short explanation of what the artist wants from the station

After Submission

What the process can look like after music is sent in

This gives your station a cleaner, more premium process artists can actually understand.

Stage 01
Submission is reviewed

The station checks the record, the links, the context, and the request to see where it fits best.

Stage 02
Fit is determined

The music may fit a spotlight, interview, feature block, replay segment, or a future live programming opportunity.

Stage 03
Follow-up happens

If the record or request is a fit, the station can follow up directly through the provided contact info.

Stage 04
Content gets leveraged

Strong submissions can become part of interviews, artist reels, social content, station promos, and future segments.

For Artists

How to improve your chances

Submit complete, professional information the first time. The cleaner the package, the easier it is for a station team to take you seriously and move faster.

Do Not Forget

Quick checklist before pressing submit

  • Double-check every music or press link
  • Make sure the record title is final and correct
  • Use a real contact email you actively check
  • Include an Instagram handle for tagging and visibility
  • Clearly say what you want from the station
